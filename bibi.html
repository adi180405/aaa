Bibi Ka Maqbara

Built in 1660 by Prince Azam Shah in memory of his mother Dilras Banu Begum. It is famously known as the "Taj of the Deccan" because of its strong resemblance to the Taj Mahal.

  • Built during Aurangzeb’s rule
  • Second largest structure built by Aurangzeb
  • Made of marble and basalt
  • Designed by Ata-ullah
  • Located in Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar

A beautiful Mughal monument built in memory of Dilras Banu Begum.

History & Construction

The mausoleum was constructed between 1668 and 1669. The total cost of construction was around Rs. 6,68,203. Marble was brought from Jaipur. Though inspired by the Taj Mahal, it has its own unique architectural beauty and historical importance.

Conclusion

Bibi Ka Maqbara is a beautiful historical monument that reflects Mughal architectural style and royal emotions. Though smaller than the Taj Mahal, it remains an important symbol of heritage and love in Indian history.
