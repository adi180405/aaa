Grishneshwar Temle is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is Located near the Ellora Caves, it is the smallest of the 12 Jyotirlingas but holds immense religious significance.

Grishneshwar Temple

Ghrishneshwar Mandir, located in Verul near Aurangabad, Maharashtra, is the 12th and final Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva. This 18th-century black stone temple, rebuilt by Ahilyabai Holkar, features intricate carvings and is situated just 1 km from the Ellora Caves. It is a major pilgrimage site known for its peaceful, divine atmosphere.

Architecture

Built using red basalt stone Five-tier shikhara (tower) Beautiful carvings of Hindu gods and goddesses Large Nandi (bull) statue facing the main shrine Sanctum (Garbhagriha) houses the Shiva Lingam
